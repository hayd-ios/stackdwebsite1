Privacy Policy for Stackd

Last updated: 14.10.2024

Introduction

Welcome to Stackd, a bulk image-sharing app that values your privacy and data security. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our app. Please take a moment to read through our practices and contact us if you have any questions.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information: When you sign up for Stackd, we may collect your email address, username, and other identifying details to manage your account.
Images and Content: Stackd allows users to upload and share images in bulk. Any images you upload are stored in our Firebase database and associated with your account.
Usage Data: We collect data on how you use the app, such as when and how often you upload images, interactions within the app, and technical information (device type, IP address, etc.).
Log Data: Firebase automatically collects log data about your interactions with the app, including any crashes, performance metrics, and error reports.
2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for several purposes, including:

To manage and improve the Stackd app and its features.
To store and manage your images.
To provide customer support and respond to your inquiries.
To notify you about updates, security alerts, and any changes to our policies.
To ensure the security and proper functioning of our services.
3. How Your Information is Shared

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your data in the following scenarios:

Service Providers: We may share information with third-party service providers (such as Firebase) to manage and store your data.
Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required by law or in response to legal processes.
Business Transfers: In the event that Stackd is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.
4. Firebase and Data Storage

Stackd uses Firebase, a cloud platform by Google, for data storage and management. Firebase ensures that your data is securely stored, but your use of Stackd also means your data is subject to Firebase’s own privacy policies. You can read more about Firebase’s security practices here.

5. Data Security

We are committed to ensuring the security of your data. We implement industry-standard security measures, including encryption and secure communication channels, to protect your personal information and images. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Data Rights

As a user, you have rights regarding your personal data:

Access and Portability: You can request access to the information we hold about you.
Deletion: You may request that we delete your account and any associated data from Stackd.
Correction: You can update or correct any inaccurate information in your account.
To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at [Support Email].

7. Children’s Privacy

Stackd is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we discover that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will delete it immediately.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be effective immediately upon posting the updated policy within the app. Please check this page periodically to stay informed about our data practices.

9. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or your data, please contact us at info@stackdsocial.com
